Case Study

Personal Portfolio Website

Designing and developing a responsive portfolio that clearly presents my design work, skills, and professional background for internship opportunities in product and UX design.

Role

Product Designer · UX Designer · Front-End Developer

Tools

Figma · HTML · CSS · VS Code · GitHub Pages

Timeline

6 days

Outcome

Responsive portfolio for internship applications

Overview

Creating a portfolio that works like a visual resume

Context

As I began applying for design internships, I needed a portfolio that could quickly communicate who I am, what I’ve built, and how I approach design problems.

Problem

Many portfolios are visually impressive but difficult to scan quickly. Important information such as projects, skills, and contact details can get buried.

Goal

Design a clean, responsive portfolio that helps recruiters understand my value within seconds and easily explore my work.

Research Insights

What I learned from reviewing other portfolios

Before designing the site, I reviewed professional UX and product design portfolios to identify patterns that improved clarity, scanability, and credibility.

Fast scanning matters

Recruiters should understand who the designer is and what they do in just a few seconds.

Projects need clear entry points

Work samples should be easy to spot, easy to click, and easy to compare.

Navigation should stay simple

Extra complexity slows users down and distracts from the work itself.

Design should support the content

Strong spacing, hierarchy, and typography make the work feel more professional.

Process

From sketching to responsive front-end build

01

Early exploration

I began with hand sketches to explore possible page structures and content priorities before moving into digital wireframes.

02

Wireframing and layout refinement

I translated the strongest concept into a Figma wireframe and refined the arrangement of headings, projects, navigation, and call-to-action areas.

03

Visual hierarchy

I adjusted type scale, spacing, and content grouping to make the site easier to scan while keeping the design clean and professional.

04

Front-end development

I built the site in HTML and CSS, then tested and adjusted the layout to ensure it worked across different screen sizes.

Key Design Decisions

How I shaped the experience

Typography

I used bold, clear headings to establish strong hierarchy and help visitors understand my design focus immediately.

Color

A muted green-gray palette keeps the site calm and professional without competing with the project work.

Layout

The page structure moves users from introduction to projects to next steps with minimal friction, supporting quick recruiter review.

Final Outcome

A polished, responsive portfolio built to support applications

The final design combines concise writing, organized hierarchy, and responsive front-end development to present me as a strong internship candidate in product, UX, and visual design.

What I Learned

A portfolio is more than a gallery

This project helped me understand that a portfolio is not just a place to store work. It is a communication tool that shapes a recruiter’s first impression.

I learned how visual hierarchy, storytelling, and responsive design work together to make a portfolio feel both memorable and easy to use. I also came away with a stronger understanding of how to design for clarity while still showing personality.
