Project Overview

Creating a portfolio that works like a visual resume

This project involved designing and programming a clean and simple website that acts as a portfolio of my projects and skill set. It was intended to be easily scannable by recruiters so they could get a snapshot of my capabilities and quickly understand my professional direction.

The Challenge

Make it clear, fast, and responsive

The site needed to be visually clean and portray the most necessary information in a way a recruiter could understand within about 8–12 seconds. It also needed to adapt well across a variety of screen sizes and devices.

Research Insights

Understanding how recruiters evaluate portfolios

Research Questions

  • What information do recruiters look for first in a portfolio?
  • How quickly do hiring managers scan portfolio websites?
  • What makes a portfolio easy to navigate and memorable?

Key Findings

  • Recruiters typically scan portfolios in under 15 seconds
  • Clear project thumbnails encourage deeper exploration
  • Minimal navigation reduces cognitive load
  • Strong typography improves readability and hierarchy
Problem

Designing a portfolio that communicates value quickly

Recruiters reviewing portfolios need to quickly understand a designer’s skills, design thinking, and project work. Many portfolios showcase attractive visuals but fail to communicate information efficiently.

The challenge was to design a portfolio that clearly communicates professional direction, projects, and capabilities within seconds while maintaining a clean and engaging visual presentation.

Design Principles

Guiding decisions throughout the project

Scanability

Recruiters should be able to understand the designer’s focus and project work within the first 10 seconds of viewing the site.

Clarity

Information hierarchy should guide viewers smoothly through projects, skills, and contact information.

Focus on Work

Visual styling should support the portfolio projects rather than distract from them.

Design Process

From sketching to refinement

01

Early Exploration

I explored different layout directions to display the information in a clean and precise way. Sketching by hand before implementing the Figma wireframe helped me quickly decide what direction I wanted to pursue.

02

Typography & Composition

I refined the typography hierarchy and spacing to improve readability and visual balance while keeping my skills and project work prominent.

Design Decisions

Key choices behind the final design

Typography

Bold headline typography was used to establish strong hierarchy and communicate my educational background and career trajectory quickly.

Color

The green-gray palette was selected to portray clean professionalism without distracting from the design work itself.

Layout

The composition was arranged to lead recruiters from a snapshot of my career focus to selected projects and then to contact information as efficiently as possible.

Final Design

From wireframe to final responsive layout

Before building the final version of the portfolio, I created a wireframe in Figma to establish the layout hierarchy and information structure. This allowed me to quickly explore how recruiters would scan the page and where key content should appear.

After validating the layout direction, I refined the visual hierarchy, typography, and spacing before implementing the final responsive version in HTML and CSS.

Future Improvements

Opportunities for iteration

  • Conduct usability testing with recruiters and designers
  • Add interactive project previews to increase engagement
  • Implement analytics to understand visitor behavior
  • Refine responsive layouts for smaller mobile devices
Reflection

A portfolio is a long-term communication tool

This project taught me more about what recruiters and hiring personnel are looking for in a portfolio. Before creating this, I had very little understanding of what a portfolio should actually do.

I learned that a portfolio should function like a visual resume: memorable, informative, responsive, and capable of evolving over time as my skills, education, and career goals continue to grow.
