Case Study

Personal Portfolio Website

Designing and coding a portfolio website that presents my work, skills, and career direction in a clear, recruiter-friendly way.

Role

Product Designer · UX Designer · Front-End Developer

Tools

Figma · HTML · CSS · VS Code · GitHub Pages

Timeline

6 Days

Focus

Responsive portfolio for internship applications

Overview

Creating a portfolio that works like a visual resume

As I began applying for internships, I needed a portfolio that could quickly explain who I am, what I design, and how I think. I wanted it to feel polished and professional, while still being simple enough for recruiters to scan in seconds.

Instead of using a template, I designed and developed my own site so it could reflect both my visual design sensibilities and my growing front-end skills.

Problem

Most portfolios are either too busy or too vague

Many portfolios showcase work beautifully, but they do not always communicate the designer’s value quickly. Since recruiters often review sites very fast, I needed mine to prioritize clarity, hierarchy, and easy navigation.

Goal

Design for quick understanding

Communicate my design focus immediately
Make projects easy to preview and explore
Keep the layout clean and responsive
Show both design and front-end ability
Research Insights

What I noticed from reviewing other portfolios

I looked at product and UX portfolios to understand what made them effective. The strongest examples made resumes easy to access, kept projects highly visible, and used spacing and typography to support readability instead of overwhelming the viewer.

Fast scanning matters

Visitors should understand the designer’s focus within seconds.

Projects need clear entry points

Work should be easy to spot, click, and compare.

Navigation should stay simple

Extra complexity distracts from the actual work.

Visual design should support content

Typography, space, and hierarchy should guide attention.

Process

From sketching to development

01

Sketch exploration

I began with hand sketches to quickly test layout directions and content flow.

02

Wireframing in Figma

I translated the strongest ideas into a digital structure and refined the content hierarchy.

03

Visual refinement

I adjusted typography, spacing, and composition to improve readability and flow.

04

HTML/CSS build

I developed the final design in code and made responsive adjustments for different screens.

Design Decisions

Key choices behind the final design

Typography

Bold headlines create clear hierarchy and immediately communicate my professional direction.

Color

A muted green-gray palette keeps the site calm, polished, and easy to read.

Layout

The structure guides recruiters from introduction to projects to next steps with minimal friction.

Final Outcome

A portfolio designed to support applications

The final site combines clear writing, visual hierarchy, and responsive front-end development to present my work in a way that feels professional, approachable, and easy to scan.

Reflection

A portfolio is a communication tool

This project taught me that a portfolio is not just a place to store work. It is a tool for shaping first impressions, communicating strengths quickly, and telling a cohesive story about where I am now and where I am headed as a designer.
