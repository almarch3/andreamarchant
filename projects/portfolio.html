Case Study

Portfolio of Andrea Marchant

Designing and programming a web portfolio for internship opportunities in UX and Product Designer roles to further my education and experience.

Role:Product Designer
Tools:Figma | GitHub | Visual Studio Code | HTML/CSS
Timeline:6 Days
Type:Portfolio to display my skill set for hiring personnel.

Project Overview

This project involved designing and programming a clean and simple website that acts as a portfolio of my projects and skill set. It was intended to be easily scannable by recruiters to get a snapshot of my capabilites with standout information.

The Challenge

The site needed to be visually clean and portray necessary information that a recruiter could scan and get the gist of me as an applicant within about 8-12 seconds. It also needed to be responsive to a variety of screens dependent on what the user chose to use.

Research & Inspiration

I looked into typical portfolio websites of UX/Product Designers for companies like Walmart, Motorola Solutions, Noggin, and Netflix. The main theme I noticed was their Resumes were easily found and able to be downloaded, and that their projects were quickly scannable. They also all displayed big companies they had done work for as well as easily accessible case studies.

Another feature I realized in researching these was the use of colors and space in each portfolio. The design did not detract from the information but rather showcased their work in creative ways. However, there were a couple of items on each portfolio, that myself as a user, disliked or was distracted by. I wanted to keep my portfolio even cleaner and simpler to read more as a quick intern resume for consideration, rather than a showcase of years of professional experience.

Design Process

Early Exploration

I explored different layout directions to display the information in a clean and precise way. Sketching by hand before implementing in the Figma wireframe helped me to more quickly get an idea of what I wanted to design.

Typography & Composition

I refined the typography hierarchy and spacing to improve readability and visual balance while keeping the overall information of my skills prevalent.

Design Decisions

Typography

Bold headline typography was used to establish strong hierarchy and communicate my educational background and career trajectory. Viewers would be able to recognize within a couple seconds what profession I was in.

Color

The green/gray palette was selected to portray clean professionalism that did not distract from the design products.

Layout

The composition was arranged to lead recruiters from a snapshot of my career focus to the selected projects and then to my contact information. Efficiency to save time was implemented, allowing recruiters to quickly understand what I do.

Final Design

The final design combines visual impact with organized hierarchy to create a polished and professional portfolio of a Pruduct Design | UX student.

Reflection

This project taught me more about what recruiters and hiring personnel at companies are looking in a portfolio. Prior to creating this, I had no clue what a portfolio should look like or be like. I learned my portfolio should be a visual resume that is memorable and informational. Upon reflection, a portfolio is a long-term project that can be adjusted based on my experiences, education, and career goals. Through UX research I learned more about the importance of interactive and responsive designs as well as storytelling to the consumer.
